# Design Directions

Use this file when the user wants a new look, stronger taste, or a clearer aesthetic.

## Pick One Direction

Choose one of these directions and carry it through typography, color, spacing, and motion:

- Editorial: Large type, confident whitespace, sharp contrast, restrained color, magazine-like composition.
- Cinematic: Dark or atmospheric surfaces, dramatic gradients or lighting, oversized focal art, deliberate motion.
- Technical: Dense information blocks, crisp dividers, compact spacing, mono accents, precise alignment.
- Warm premium: Soft neutrals, tactile surfaces, elegant serif or humanist pairings, subtle depth.
- Playful bold: Saturated accent color, exaggerated scale shifts, rounded geometry, lively but controlled motion.

## Turn Direction Into Decisions

For any chosen direction, define:

- headline and body type pairing
- primary background treatment
- accent and neutral color roles
- component shape language
- section rhythm
- motion character

## Quick Heuristics

- Marketing pages: Bias toward memorable hero treatment and section pacing.
- Dashboards: Bias toward clarity, scanning speed, and consistent panel structure.
- Consumer tools: Bias toward approachability and obvious primary actions.
- Creator or entertainment surfaces: Bias toward mood, contrast, and expressive typography.

If the user names a brand or reference, match the underlying principles rather than copying surface details.
